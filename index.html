Bipedal Robot Walking Control

MPC LIPM Dynamics MuJoCo Python/C++

Designed a hierarchical control framework for a bipedal robot to achieve stable walking on uneven terrains in MuJoCo simulation. Implemented a Model Predictive Controller (MPC) based on simplified dynamics to optimize foot placement in real-time.

[View Code on GitHub]
▶ Click to view Mathematical Derivation (LIPM & MPC)

We model the robot using the **Linear Inverted Pendulum Mode (LIPM)** dynamics:

$$ \ddot{x} = \frac{g}{z_c} (x - p_x) $$

Where $x$ is the CoM position, $z_c$ is constant height, and $p_x$ is the ZMP.

The MPC optimization problem is formulated as a quadratic program (QP):

$$ \min_{U} \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} \| X_{k+1} - X_{ref} \|_Q^2 + \| U_k \|_R^2 $$

Subject to dynamics constraints $X_{k+1} = AX_k + BU_k$ and ZMP stability polygons.
